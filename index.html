The Foundation

Every Member Agrees to Three Laws

Violation of any of these three laws results in immediate removal from the Alliance. These are not guidelines — they are the standard.

I

Use AI Legally

No automation of illegal activity. No facilitation of fraud. No use of AI to deceive, manipulate, or harm. Compliance with AIVIA, BIPA, HIPAA, SB 2427, and all applicable law.

II

Use AI Ethically

Respect human dignity, privacy, and autonomy. Be honest about what AI does and does not do. What you cannot explain, you cannot deploy.

III

Use AI Morally

Build systems that make humans stronger, not dependent. Create value for all stakeholders. Human dignity cannot be legislated — only chosen.

"Strong humans build smart systems. Smart systems do not replace strong humans. They extend them." — The Human Principle, Block by Block AI Safety & Security Alliance

Art. I
Name & Purpose — Establish and enforce AI standards; provide education and certification; govern Club entry systems; create scalable AI safety models for organizations and government.
Art. II
The Three Laws — Legal (comply with all applicable law), Ethical (fair, transparent, auditable), Moral (serve human dignity and the broader good). These are immutable.
Art. III
Club Entry System — Strong Humans Club (professionals/leaders), Investment Club (accredited investors), Polish-American Business & Investment Club (cultural community). No territorial exclusivity.
Art. IV
Membership Tiers & Chapters — Standard Member, Founding Member, Trusted Service Provider, Geographic Chapters (non-exclusive, voluntary), Hub Operating License (separate license required).
Art. V
Operating Standards — AI used legally, ethically, with integrity. Education before deployment. Community accountability. Financial transparency in all investment discussions.
Art. VI
Certification Paths — AI Champion (4-week), AI Director (3-month), AI Consultant (full-spectrum incubator track). Delivered through the AI Impact Academy.
Art. VII
Deployment Sectors — Corporate & HR, Healthcare & Elder Care, K-12 Education, Higher Education, Government & Public Safety, Professional Services.
Art. VIII
Version Control — Versioned format v[Major].[Minor]. Current: v1.1. Minor updates: 14-day member notice. Major updates: 30-day notice + comment period.
Art. IX
Enforcement — Three violation tiers: Minor (warning → suspension → termination), Moderate (suspension → termination), Severe (immediate termination). Appeals reviewed in 60 days.
Art. X
Governing Law — Laws of the State of Illinois. Disputes: good-faith mediation in Cook County, then binding arbitration (AAA rules) if mediation fails.
